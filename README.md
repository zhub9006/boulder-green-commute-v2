# Boulder Green Commute Guide

A comprehensive guide for Boulder residents on sustainable commuting options, featuring walking and biking route analyses with detailed travel times and directions.

## About This Guide

This project provides detailed commute analyses for Boulder, Colorado residents considering greener transportation alternatives. Each analysis includes turn-by-turn directions, distance, estimated travel time, and comparison of different sustainable modes.

## Current Analyses

### 1. Walking & Biking Commute: Oak Avenue to Broadway

**Route:** 45 Oak Avenue → 1800 Broadway, Boulder, CO

| Mode | Distance | Duration | Pace |
|------|----------|----------|------|
| 🚶 Walking | 0.87 km (0.54 mi) | ~2 minutes | ~4 min/km |
| 🚲 Biking | 0.87 km (0.54 mi) | ~2 minutes | ~2.5 min/km |

#### Route Details

1. **Start** at 45 Oak Avenue
2. Head south on Oak Avenue toward 19th Street
3. Turn right onto 19th Street (heading east)
4. Turn left onto Arapahoe Avenue
5. Continue east on 17th Street
6. Turn right onto Pearl Street
7. **Arrive** at 1800 Broadway

### Benefits of This Route

- **Short Distance**: Under 1 km makes both walking and biking highly practical
- **Flat Terrain**: Boulder's relatively flat downtown core is ideal for cycling
- **Bike-Friendly**: Multiple bike lanes and shared-use paths available
- **Walkable**: Well-maintained sidewalks throughout
- **Time Efficient**: Both modes are faster than driving in Boulder's downtown traffic

## Why Choose Sustainable Commuting?

### Environmental Impact
- **Walking**: Zero emissions, zero energy consumption
- **Biking**: Zero emissions, minimal energy consumption
- A 1 km drive produces approximately 200g of CO₂

### Health Benefits
- **Walking**: Improves cardiovascular health, strengthens muscles, boosts mood
- **Biking**: Excellent cardio workout, builds leg strength, low-impact exercise

### Economic Benefits
- Saves on gas, parking, and vehicle maintenance
- Reduces need for car ownership or secondary vehicles
